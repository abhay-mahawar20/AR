Augmented Reality

What is augmented reality


Augmented Reality (AR) is a technology that overlays digital information—such as
images, sounds, or videos—onto the real world, enhancing the user's perception of their environment.

Interactive Experience


AR allows users to interact with digital elements in real-time, creating a more immersive experience.

Enhanced Learning


AR can be used in education to provide interactive learning experiences, making complex subjects easier to understand.

Real-World Applications


From gaming to healthcare, AR has a wide range of applications that enhance everyday life.




How Augmented Reality works


Hardware : AR typically requires devices with cameras and sensors, such as smartphones, tablets, or AR glasses (e.g., Microsoft HoloLens, Google Glass).


Software : AR applications use computer vision, simultaneous localization and mapping (SLAM), and depth tracking to identify real-world objects and overlay digital content accurately.







Types of Augmented Reality

Marker-based AR

Utilizes specific visual markers to trigger augmented experiences.

Markerless AR:

Uses sensors and GPS to determine the user's location.

Location-based AR:

Integrates digital information based on the user's geographical location.

Projection-based AR:

Projects digital images directly onto physical surfaces.

Superimposition-based AR:

Replaces or enhances the original view of an object with an augmented view.

Contour-based AR:

Recognizes and interacts with the shapes and edges of objects in the environment
