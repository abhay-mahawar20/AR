What is Augmented Reality (AR)?

Augmented reality (AR) is a technology that overlays digital informationâ€”such as images, sounds, or videosâ€”onto the real world, enhancing the user's perception of their environment.

Types of Augmented Reality

  • Marker-based AR: Utilizes specific visual markers to trigger augmented experiences.
  • Markerless AR: Uses sensors and GPS to determine the user's location.
  • Location-based AR: Integrates digital information based on the user's geographical location.
  • Projection-based AR: Projects digital images directly onto physical surfaces.
  • Superimposition-based AR: Replaces or enhances the original view of an object with an augmented view.
  • Contour-based AR: Recognizes and interacts with the shapes and edges of objects in the environment.
